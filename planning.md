# Provenance Guard — Planning Document

## Detection Signals

### Signal 1: LLM Classification (Groq)
- **What it measures:** Holistic semantic and stylistic assessment of whether text reads as AI-generated
- **Output format:** A score between 0–1 where 1 = definitely AI
- **Blind spots:** Polished or formal human writing (academic essays, structured arguments) can pattern-match to AI writing and score high incorrectly

### Signal 2: Stylometric Heuristics
- **What it measures:** Statistical properties of text — sentence length variance, type-token ratio (vocabulary diversity), punctuation density
- **Output format:** A score between 0–1 where 1 = definitely AI
- **Blind spots:** Poetry (short, repetitive, simple lines) and non-native English speakers (uniform, careful sentence structure) both produce low variance that mimics AI writing

### Combining Signals
- **Weights:** 60% stylometric, 40% LLM
- **Justification:** The LLM is more likely to confidently label something AI-generated, so weighting stylometrics slightly higher reduces false positive risk

## Uncertainty Representation
- **Spread threshold:** If `abs(llm_score - stylometric_score) > 0.30`, output uncertain regardless of individual scores
- **Label thresholds:**
  - 0.75 and above → Likely AI
  - 0.45 to 0.74 → Uncertain
  - Below 0.45 → Likely Human
- **Justification for asymmetry:** False positives (calling human work AI) are worse than false negatives. The high bar of 0.75 to call something AI reflects that cost asymmetry, while the lower bar of 0.45 to call something human is more forgiving by design

## Transparency Labels
| Scenario | Label Text |
|----------|------------|
| High-confidence AI | "Our system has determined this content was likely generated by AI with high confidence." |
| High-confidence Human | "Our system has determined this content was likely written by a human with high confidence." |
| Uncertain | "Our system couldn't confidently determine whether this content was AI-generated or human-written. Take this attribution with caution." |

## Appeals Workflow
1. POST /appeal receives `content_id` and `creator_reasoning`
2. Look up the original submission by `content_id`
3. Update its status to `under_review`
4. Log the appeal reasoning alongside the original classification entry
5. Return confirmation that the appeal was received

## Edge Cases
1. **Poetry:** Short, repetitive, simple lines score as low variance on stylometrics, triggering a false AI positive despite being intentional artistic choices
2. **Non-native English speakers:** Careful, uniform sentence structure to avoid mistakes produces low sentence length variance, which stylometrics interprets as AI-like

## Architecture

### Submission Flow

[Client]

→ POST /submit (text, creator_id)

→ Generate content_id

→ Groq LLM Signal (text) → llm_score (0-1)

→ Stylometric Signal (text) → stylometric_score (0-1)

→ Scoring: check spread, apply weights → confidence score

→ Label assignment based on thresholds → label text

→ Audit Log (content_id, creator_id, timestamp, llm_score,

stylometric_score, confidence, attribution, status)

→ Response (content_id, attribution, confidence, label)

### Appeal Flow

[Client]

→ POST /appeal (content_id, creator_reasoning)

→ Lookup original submission by content_id

→ Update status to under_review

→ Audit Log (append appeal_reasoning, updated status)

→ Response (confirmation)
